# **Industrial Product Definition (IPD)**

## **1 | What is IPD**

IPD (Industrial Product Definition) is used to define:

which structural combinations generated from SCF constitute valid industrial product existence.

Within HKB Structure Engine:

IPD belongs to the industrial existence layer.

SCF defines structural dimensions.

IPD determines whether combinations of those dimensions can form real industrial products.

---

## **2 | Why IPD Is Needed**

SCF solves the problem of structural dimension definition.

However:

SCF itself does not determine whether every structural combination is valid.

For example:

certain structural combinations may theoretically exist through dimensional permutation,

but in the real industrial world they may:

- fail engineering requirements
- lack supply chain support
- possess no practical application logic
- contain structural contradictions
- lack long-term sustainability

Therefore:

after SCF,

an industrial existence determination layer becomes necessary.

This layer is IPD.

---

## **3 | The Nature of IPD**

IPD is not:

- a SKU system
- a website categorization system
- a marketplace structure
- a listing architecture

Instead:

IPD is:

a structural existence determination system for industrial products.

Its purpose is to determine:

whether structural combinations generated by SCF can form valid industrial existence.

---

## **4 | How IPD Is Formed**

IPD originates from permutations and combinations of SCF dimensions.

For example:

if a product series contains:

Emission System:
SMD / COB

Maintenance System:
Front Service / Rear Service

then the theoretical combinations may include:

SMD + Front Service
SMD + Rear Service
COB + Front Service
COB + Rear Service

However:

not every combination necessarily forms a valid industrial product.

Therefore:

IPD performs industrial validity determination.

---

## **5 | Core Determination Logic of IPD**

IPD contains only two determination results:

| **Result** | **Meaning** |
| --- | --- |
| Valid | The structural combination constitutes real industrial product existence |
| Invalid | The structural combination does not constitute real industrial product existence |

## **6 | What “Valid” Means**

“Valid” does not necessarily mean:

- currently mass-produced
- currently sold on the market
- currently commercialized

Instead:

it means the structure possesses:

- engineering validity
- supply chain validity
- structural consistency
- application feasibility
- long-term industrial sustainability

Therefore:

it belongs to valid industrial structural existence.

---

## **7 | What “Invalid” Means**

“Invalid” means:

although the structure may theoretically appear through dimensional combination,

it does not satisfy the conditions required for real industrial existence.

Possible reasons include:

- engineering contradictions
- structural conflicts
- unstable supply chains
- invalid application scenarios
- long-term unsustainability

Therefore:

the structure does not belong to real industrial product existence.

---

## **8 | Difference Between IPD and Traditional Product Systems**

Traditional product systems often assume:

If a SKU exists,
then the product exists.

However:

HKB Structure Engine believes:

the existence of a SKU does not determine whether an industrial structure is valid.

Because a SKU may merely represent:

- temporary market behavior
- website organization logic
- e-commerce operational strategy
- parameter variation

IPD focuses instead on:

whether the industrial structure itself is valid.

---

## **9 | One of the Most Important Values of IPD**

One of the greatest values of IPD is:

the ability to identify industrial product structures that should exist but do not yet exist commercially.

Once SCF dimensions are established,

the system naturally generates large numbers of structural combinations.

Among them:

certain combinations may not yet exist in the market,

but may already possess:

- engineering feasibility
- application feasibility
- supply chain feasibility

This means:

they represent potentially valid industrial product directions.

Therefore:

IPD is not only a product definition system.

It is also:

an industrial product direction inference system.

---

## **10 | IPD and AI**

In the AI era:

AI systems must not only understand:

what products currently exist.

They must also understand:

what structures are capable of existing.

IPD provides:

the existence logic of the industrial product world.

Compared with traditional SKU systems,

this logic is much closer to real industrial reality.

Therefore:

IPD can help AI systems establish:

- industrial structural inference capability
- product validity judgment capability
- structural prediction capability
- industrial evolution reasoning capability

---

## **11 | Relationship Between IPD and MAP**

IPD belongs to the industrial existence layer.

MAP belongs to the channel mapping layer.

Therefore:

MAP can only map structures that have already been validated by IPD.

MAP cannot reversely define IPD.

This means:

websites,

marketplaces,

and channels

do not possess industrial product definition authority.

---

## **12 | The Goal of IPD**

The core goal of IPD is:

to establish an industrial existence determination system for the industrial product world.

It defines not:

- SKUs
- website products
- listing structures
- marketplace categories

but rather:

which structural combinations constitute real industrial product existence.

---

## **13 | Conclusion**

SCF defines:

the structural dimensions of the industrial world.

IPD defines:

which structural combinations are truly valid.

Therefore:

IPD represents the critical layer through which the structural world enters the world of real industrial existence.
