# SCF

Structure Classification Framework for defining industrial structural dimensions.

*Published by HUAKE LIMITED*

*Framework Author: Ethan Zhang*

*Theory System: HKB Structure Engine*

*First Public Release: May 2026*

## **1｜What is SCF**

SCF (Structure Classification Framework) is:

### **A structural classification framework.**

SCF is used to define:

the structural dimension system of the industrial product world.

Within HKB Structure Engine:

SCF belongs to:

### **The world structure layer.**

SCF does not define:

- SKUs
- Website categories
- Listings
- ERP codes
- Product parameters

Instead, it defines:

### **The structural modes of existence within the industrial product world.**

---

## **2｜Why SCF Is Needed**

In traditional industrial product systems:

product structures are often mixed together with:

- SKUs
- Parameters
- Listings
- Website categories
- ERP codes

As a result:

industrial products lack a unified structural representation.

For example:

a single industrial product structure may generate numerous SKUs because of differences in:

- Size
- Voltage
- Power
- Packaging
- Website
- Platform

However, many of these differences:

do not belong to:

### **Essential structural differences.**

Therefore:

HKB Structure Engine introduces:

the SCF layer.

Its purpose is:

### **To separate industrial structures from the parameter world.**

---

## **3｜The Nature of SCF**

SCF is not:

a product categorization system.

Instead, it is:

### **An industrial structural dimension system.**

SCF defines:

which differences within the industrial product world belong to:

### **Structural differences.**

For example:

- Emission systems
- Control systems
- Installation systems
- Maintenance systems
- Display systems
- Packaging systems

These belong to:

### **World structural differences.**

Whereas:

- Power
- Size
- Voltage
- Length
- Pixel pitch
- Color
- Packaging quantity

typically belong to:

### **Parameter differences.**

---

## **4｜The Categorization World and the Structural World**

HKB Structure Engine believes that:

within the industrial product world,

there exist:

### **The categorization world**

and:

### **The structural world.**

---

## **4.1 The Categorization World**

The categorization world is used to establish:

high-level product organizational relationships.

For example:

LED
→ LED Display
→ LED Modules
→ Indoor LED Modules

At this stage:

products mainly belong to:

### **Categorization systems.**

---

## **4.2 The Structural World**

Once products enter:

a stable industrial structural stage,

they begin entering:

### **The structural world.**

For example: Indoor LED Modules

At this point:

structural differences such as:

- Emission systems
- Maintenance systems
- Packaging systems
- Installation systems

begin to emerge.

This is where:

SCF begins to operate.

---

# **5｜Structure Nodes**

## **5.1 What Is a Structure Node**

A structure node is:

### **The starting node where the categorization world enters the structural world.**

Before this node:

products mainly follow:

categorization logic.

After this node:

products begin entering:

industrial structural logic.

---

## **5.2 Example of a Structure Node**

For example:

LED
→ LED Display
→ LED Modules
→ Indoor LED Modules
→ SMD Indoor LED Modules
→ 320×160 SMD Indoor LED Modules
→ Front Service 320×160 SMD Indoor LED Modules

Among these: Indoor LED Modules

is typically a more suitable structure node for establishing SCF.

Because at this stage:

industrial structures begin to stabilize.

---

## **5.3 Structure Node Granularity**

SCF nodes do not have an absolute standard.

In different industries:

the most appropriate SCF node usually requires joint judgment based on:

- Industry experience
- Engineering experience
- Supply chain cognition
- Customer cognition

---

## **5.4 Problems with Overly Broad Nodes**

If the structure node is too broad:

for example: LED Display

then:

the structural differences become too large,

causing SCF dimensions to lose stability.

---

## **5.5 Problems with Overly Detailed Nodes**

If the structure node is too detailed:

for example: 320×160 Front Service SMD Module

then:

the parameter world begins contaminating the structural world.

SCF gradually degenerates into:

a SKU system.

---

## **5.6 The Goal of Structure Nodes**

The core goal of a structure node is:

to find:

### **The most stable industrial structural abstraction layer**

between:

### **The categorization world**

and:

### **The parameter world.**

---

## **6｜Core Components of SCF**

SCF consists of:

### **Dimensions**

and:

### **Dimension Values.**

---

## **6.1 Dimensions**

Dimensions are used to define:

structural directions within the industrial product world.

For example:

| **Dimension** | **Examples** |
| --- | --- |
| Emission System | SMD / COB |
| Installation System | Standard / Quick |
| Control System | AS / CL |
| Maintenance System | Front / Rear |

Dimensions themselves represent:

### **Structural observation directions.**

---

## **6.2 Dimension Values**

Dimension values define:

specific structural existence forms.

For example:

| **Dimension** | **Dimension Value** |
| --- | --- |
| Emission System | SMD |
| Emission System | COB |
| Installation System | Quick |
| Control System | Cloud Control |

Dimension values represent:

### **Real structural existence forms.**

---

## **7｜Primary and Secondary Dimensions**

Within SCF:

dimensions are typically divided into:

| **Type** | **Essence** |
| --- | --- |
| Primary Dimensions | Define essential product structures |
| Secondary Dimensions | Define structural expression forms |

## **7.1 Primary Dimensions**

Primary dimensions typically determine:

the core structural differences within the product world.

For example:

within LED modules:

- SMD
- GOB
- COB

represent essential emission packaging differences.

Therefore:

they belong to:

primary dimensions.

---

## **7.2 Secondary Dimensions**

Secondary dimensions typically determine:

structural expression forms.

For example:

- Front Service
- Rear Service
- Top Emitting
- Side Emitting

These usually do not change the essential product world,

but instead change:

the structural representation form.

Therefore:

they belong to:

secondary dimensions.

---

# **8｜Principles for Determining Structural Dimensions**

Primary dimensions within SCF are not determined by a single role.

Instead:

they are formed through long-term collective cognition from:

- Manufacturing
- Customers
- Engineering
- Application scenarios
- Supply chains

---

## **8.1 Reference Factors for Primary Dimensions**

| **Factor** | **Description** |
| --- | --- |
| Manufacturing Influence | Degree of influence on production structures |
| Customer Influence | Degree of influence on customer cognition |
| Application Influence | Degree of influence on application scenarios |
| Long-Term Stability | Whether the structure exists stably over time |
| Manufacturing Cognition | Long-term recognition from manufacturers |
| Customer Cognition | Long-term recognition from customers |
| Engineering Cognition | Long-term recognition from engineering systems |
| Supply Chain Cognition | Whether stable supply chain systems exist |

---

## **8.2 Primary Dimensions Are Not Parameter Categories**

Only differences that simultaneously possess:

- Long-term stability
- Industry-wide collective cognition
- Industrial structural independence

are suitable for entering:

the SCF primary dimension system.

---

## **8.3 Primary Dimensions Are Not Necessarily Defined by Manufacturers**

Some structural differences:

may not be considered important by manufacturers.

However:

if they possess:

- Long-term customer recognition
- Long-term engineering recognition
- Long-term application stability
- Long-term market existence

then they may still become:

SCF primary dimensions.

---

## **9｜The Difference Between Attributes and Dimensions**

Within SCF:

not every difference becomes a dimension.

This is one of the most important principles of SCF.

---

## **9.1 What Becomes a Dimension**

Only differences that are:

### **Long-term stable,**

### **Repeatable,**

### **Industrially meaningful**

can become:

SCF dimensions.

---

## **9.2 What Belongs to Attributes**

The following usually belong to attributes:

- Size
- Power
- Voltage
- Pixel pitch
- Length
- Color
- Installation accessories
- Control protocols

These belong to:

### **The parameter world.**

Rather than:

### **The structural world.**

---

## **10｜Expansion Rules of SCF**

SCF is not an infinitely expanding system.

New dimensions must satisfy:

- Long-term structural stability
- Long-term supply chain existence
- Clear industrial essence
- Ability to form an independent structural world

For example:

new:

- Emission systems
- Display systems
- Installation systems

may become new dimensions.

However:

pure parameter changes

must not become dimensions.

---

## **11｜The Relationship Between SCF and Channels**

SCF belongs to:

### **The industrial world layer.**

Therefore:

websites,

platforms,

and channels

must not reversely define:

SCF structures.

For example:

SCF dimensions must not be created merely because of:

- SEO requirements
- Website categorization
- Advertising strategies

---

## **12｜The Goal of SCF**

The core goal of SCF is:

to establish:

### **A unified structural language for the industrial product world.**

This language does not depend on:

- Websites
- SKUs
- ERP systems
- Listings
- E-commerce platforms

Instead:

it is used to uniformly represent:

structural existence relationships within the industrial product world.

---

## **13｜Conclusion**

SCF is:

the starting point of HKB Structure Engine.

It defines not:

product categories,

but rather:

### **The structural rules of the industrial product world.**

Within SCF:

products first belong to:

the structural world.

Only afterwards do they enter:

- The industrial existence layer (IPD)
- The channel mapping layer (MAP)
- The visual representation layer (VIS)
