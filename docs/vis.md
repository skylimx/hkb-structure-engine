# **Visual Identity Structure (VIS)**

## **1 | What is VIS**

VIS (Visual Identity Structure) is the visual cognition system of industrial structures.

VIS defines:

how industrial product structures form:

- visual systems
- page presentation systems
- rendering systems
- banner systems
- scenario systems
- visual cognition systems

Within HKB Structure Engine:

VIS belongs to the visual representation layer.

---

## **2 | Why VIS Is Needed**

Industrial product structures do not automatically produce unified visual cognition.

For example:

the same IPD structure may adopt completely different:

- visual styles
- scenario expressions
- visual priorities
- product perception methods

across different channels.

Therefore:

even if industrial structures have already been validated,

and channel mappings have already been established,

users ultimately interact with:

the visual world.

VIS exists to establish:

the relationship between:

- industrial structures
- and visual cognition.

---

## **3 | The Nature of VIS**

VIS is not:

- an image management system
- a design asset system
- a media library system

Instead:

VIS is:

a visual representation system for industrial structures.

VIS defines:

how an industrial structure should be visually understood by users.

---

## **4 | The Foundation of VIS**

VIS must be established on top of:

- MAP
or
- IPD

This means:

VIS cannot exist independently from industrial structures.

Therefore:

the structural relationship becomes:

SCF
→ IPD
→ MAP
→ VIS

Where:

- SCF defines structural dimensions
- IPD defines industrial existence
- MAP defines channel mappings
- VIS defines visual representation

---

## **5 | Why VIS Must Exist Independently**

Traditional systems often assume:

Images = page assets

However:

HKB Structure Engine believes:

visual systems are not merely page resources.

Because visual systems directly influence:

- product cognition
- user understanding
- engineering judgment
- product positioning
- application cognition

Therefore:

visual systems themselves belong to:

industrial structural cognition.

---

## **6 | The Relationship Between VIS and Industrial Structures**

VIS does not define industrial product structures.

VIS can only express structures that have already been validated.

Therefore:

VIS cannot modify:

- SCF
- IPD
- MAP

due to:

- page design
- advertising style
- artistic preference
- rendering requirements

This means:

VIS belongs to:

the representation layer,

rather than:

the definition layer.

---

## **7 | Binding Methods of VIS**

VIS usually supports two binding methods:

| **Binding Method** | **Meaning** |
| --- | --- |
| MAP Binding | Corresponds to a specific channel representation |
| IPD Binding | Corresponds to a specific industrial structure itself |

## **8 | Why MAP Binding Is Needed**

Certain visual content belongs to:

channel-specific representation.

Examples include:

- website banners
- collection headers
- SEO page visuals
- marketplace visual systems

These contents usually depend on:

the MAP structure of a specific channel.

Therefore:

VIS must support MAP binding.

---

## **9 | Why IPD Binding Is Needed**

Certain visual content belongs to:

the industrial structure itself.

Examples include:

- industrial renderings
- engineering structure diagrams
- exploded structure diagrams
- structural standard illustrations

These contents do not depend on specific websites.

Instead:

they depend on real industrial structures.

Therefore:

VIS must support IPD binding.

---

## **10 | Core Components of VIS**

VIS structures usually contain:

| **Component** | **Description** |
| --- | --- |
| Binding Type | MAP or IPD |
| Binding Target | Related structural object |
| Visual Type | Banner / Render / Scenario / Diagram, etc. |
| Visual Resource | Images, videos, renderings, etc. |
| Visual Description | Explanation of the visual representation logic |

## **11 | VIS and AI**

In the AI era:

AI systems must not only understand industrial structures.

They must also understand:

how industrial structures form user cognition.

VIS provides:

the relationship layer between industrial structures and visual cognition.

Therefore:

AI systems can understand:

- why different websites use different visual systems
- why the same structure may possess different visual expressions
- why certain visuals belong to channel representation
- why certain visuals belong to industrial structures themselves

---

## **12 | The Goal of VIS**

The core goal of VIS is:

to establish a unified visual cognition system for the industrial product world.

VIS does not solve:

- whether products are valid
- how channels organize products

Instead:

VIS solves:

how industrial structures form stable visual cognition.

---

## **13 | Conclusion**

SCF defines:

industrial structural dimensions.

IPD defines:

which structures are valid.

MAP defines:

how industrial structures enter the channel world.

VIS defines:

how industrial structures enter the world of visual cognition.

Therefore:

VIS represents the critical layer through which the industrial structural world enters the visual cognition world.
