# **Introduction to HKB Structure Engine**

Introduction to the philosophy and structural worldview of HKB Structure Engine.

*Published by HUAKE LIMITED*

*Framework Author: Ethan Zhang*

*Theory System: HKB Structure Engine*

*First Public Release: May 2026*

## **1｜Why a Structure Engine Is Needed**

In traditional industrial product systems, products are typically represented directly as SKUs, listings, website categories, or ERP codes.

This approach was sufficient during the early stages of digitalization. However, as product complexity, channel complexity, and AI participation continue to increase, traditional product representation methods have begun to expose significant limitations.

Typical problems include:

- The same product being repeatedly redefined across different websites
- Website structures reversely influencing product structures
- SKU explosion
- Product attributes being mixed with product essence
- Industrial structures lacking stable representation
- AI systems being unable to truly understand the product world
- Lack of a unified structural language across systems

In traditional systems, the concept of “product” is often forced to simultaneously承担 multiple responsibilities, including:

- Industrial structure
- Channel representation
- Business strategy
- Page presentation
- SKU parameters
- SEO structure

As a result:

the industrial product world gradually loses structural stability.

---

## **2｜Problems in Traditional Industrial Product Systems**

### **2.1 SKU Is Not the Product**

In most ERP, PIM, and e-commerce systems:

Product = SKU

However, in reality:

a SKU is merely a parameterized transaction instance of a product.

It does not represent:

- The essential product structure
- The industrial implementation method
- Structural existence in the product world
- Supply chain structural relationships

For example:

a single industrial product structure may generate a large number of SKUs due to variations such as:

- Power
- Size
- Voltage
- Color
- Packaging
- Channel

But these SKU variations do not necessarily mean they belong to different product worlds.

---

### **2.2 Listings Are Not Product Structures**

In traditional e-commerce systems:

website listings often reversely define products.

For example:

- Creating new “products” for SEO purposes
- Splitting structures for advertising
- Redefining products according to channel rules
- Reconstructing product definitions based on platform attributes

As a result:

channel structures begin to contaminate industrial structures.

The product itself loses a unified definition.

---

### **2.3 AI Cannot Understand the Industrial Product World**

In the AI era, the problems of traditional product systems become even more severe.

This is because AI cannot truly understand:

- The SKU world
- The listing world
- Chaotic attribute systems

What AI truly requires is:

an industrial product world with:

- Stable structures
- Clear hierarchies
- Explicit relationships

Without a unified structural representation system,

AI can only process fragmented data,

rather than develop genuine industrial cognition.

---

## **3｜The Nature of the Industrial Product World**

HKB Structure Engine believes that:

the industrial product world is not:

**A collection of SKUs**

Nor is it:

**A collection of website products**

Instead, it is:

# **A world of structural existence.**

A product is first:

a form of industrial structural existence.

Only afterwards does it become:

- A website entity
- A business entity
- A SKU entity
- A page entity
- A visual entity

Therefore:

the industrial product world requires:

a structural representation system that is independent of:

- Channels
- Websites
- SKUs

---

## **4｜The Core Objective of HKB Structure Engine**

The core objective of HKB Structure Engine is:

to establish a structured representation model for the industrial product world in the AI era.

This model is used to:

- Define industrial product structures
- Define modes of product existence
- Define relationships between structures
- Define channel mapping methods
- Define visual representation methods
- Establish a unified industrial structural language

HKB Structure Engine is not:

- An ERP system
- A PIM system
- A website categorization system
- A SKU system
- An e-commerce backend

Instead, it belongs to:

# **The industrial product cognition layer.**

---

## **5｜Core Layers of HKB Structure Engine**

HKB Structure Engine currently consists of four core layers:

| **Layer** | **Name** | **Essence** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| SCF | Structure Classification Framework | World structure rules |
| IPD | Industrial Product Definition | Industrial existence structures |
| MAP | Marketplace Mapping | Channel mapping structures |
| VIS | Visual Identity Structure | Visual representation structures |

### **5.1 SCF**

SCF is used to define:

structural dimensions within the industrial product world.

Examples include:

- Emission systems
- Installation systems
- Control systems
- Maintenance systems
- Display systems

SCF belongs to:

the world structure layer.

---

### **5.2 IPD**

IPD is used to define:

industrially existing product structures generated from SCF.

IPD includes industrial existence states such as:

- Existing
- Planned
- World-Existing
- Invalid

IPD belongs to:

the industrial reality layer.

---

### **5.3 MAP**

MAP is used to define:

how industrial structures are mapped into websites and channels.

MAP allows:

different websites to adopt different structural expressions.

However:

channels are not allowed to reversely define industrial structures.

---

### **5.4 VIS**

VIS is used to define:

visual and presentation-layer representations of products.

VIS can bind to:

- MAP
- IPD

VIS belongs to:

the visual cognition layer of industrial structures.

---

## **6｜The Structured Industrial World in the AI Era**

In the AI era:

industrial products are no longer merely:

“product data.”

Instead, they are increasingly becoming:

a foundational layer for AI to understand the real industrial world.

Therefore:

the industrial product world requires:

- Stable structures
- Clear hierarchies
- Unified language
- Explicit boundaries

HKB Structure Engine attempts to establish:

a structural language for industrial products that can be jointly understood by:

- Humans
- Systems
- AI

---

## **7｜Conclusion**

HKB Structure Engine does not attempt to redefine:

ERP systems,

SKUs,

websites,

or e-commerce platforms.

Instead:

it establishes:

an independent industrial product structural world above these systems.

In this world:

products first exist as structural entities,

and only afterwards become:

- Transaction entities
- Page entities
- Channel entities
- Visual entities
